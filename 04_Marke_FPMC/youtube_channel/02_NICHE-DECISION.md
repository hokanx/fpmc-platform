# Niche Decision — Kids vs. Gaming vs. Education

Candidates raised: **kids** or **gaming**. The tutorial itself recommends
**finance / tech / education** (highest RPM). Analysis below, then the pick.

## Option A — Kids content ❌ (rejected)

| Factor | Reality (2026) |
|---|---|
| RPM | ~$1–3 per 1,000 views — "Made for Kids" videos get **only contextual ads** (no personalized ads) |
| Features lost | Comments, notification bell, end screens, cards, memberships, Super Thanks — all disabled on MFK content → no community, no funnel |
| Legal | COPPA liability is on the *creator* regardless of intent; FTC per-violation penalty reached **$53,088** in 2025 |
| Policy risk | AI-generated kids content is the **#1 target** of YouTube's 2025/26 "inauthentic content" crackdown (16 channels, 4.7B combined views, permanently terminated) |
| Fit | FPMC is a cinematic film/music brand — kids content dilutes it |

Kids is the highest-risk, lowest-reward option on the table. Rejected.

## Option B — Pure gaming (gameplay/lore with game footage) ⚠️

- Gaming CPM is low: **~$1–4** (vs. finance $25–50).
- Lore/story channels work faceless (VaatiVidya model), but using **game
  footage and copyrighted characters** creates Content ID / fair-use
  exposure — bad for a fresh channel that needs a clean monetization review.
- Hyper-competitive; discovery for new channels is brutal.

## Option C — Cinematic education (Bright Side model) ✅ but generic

Exactly what the tutorial clones. Highest RPM of the three, evergreen,
perfect fit for the Higgsfield explainer pipeline. Weakness: crowded with
identical AI clones since the tutorial went viral — differentiation needed.

## ✅ The pick: A + B hybrid is impossible, so **B × C — "Gaming History, told cinematically"**

**Gaming-themed educational documentaries**: the true, dramatic, verifiable
stories *behind* video games — crashes, cold-war smuggling, buried
cartridges, console wars — told in the Pompeii-style immersive documentary
format from the tutorial.

Why this wins:

1. **It IS the gaming niche** (satisfies the original intent) but earns
   **education-tier RPM** — the content is history/business storytelling,
   which advertisers price far above let's-plays.
2. **Zero footage risk** — stories are told with original stylized
   AI visuals, not game captures. No Content ID claims, no trademark-heavy
   imagery needed. Facts are public history (verifiable → satisfies the
   accuracy rule in the Higgsfield workflow AND YouTube's originality bar).
3. **Evergreen** — the 1983 crash never stops being true. Retention format:
   second-person hooks, mystery structure, "you are there" narration.
4. **Perfect pipeline fit** — non-photoreal stylized visuals are exactly
   what the Higgsfield video-explainer workflow enforces; a consistent
   retro-cinematic style key becomes the channel's visual signature.
5. **FPMC-adjacent** — cinematic storytelling + sound is literally the
   crew's craft. The channel can later cross-promote FPMC music/scores.
6. **Differentiated** — post-tutorial clone channels do generic "history &
   mysteries". A focused gaming-history lane has a clear identity and an
   underserved, high-affinity audience (25–40, nostalgic, high watch time).

**Audience/language:** English-first (global gaming audience, ~10× the DE
pool). DE/AR versions can be re-voiced later from the same clips — the
pipeline voices narration separately per block, so localization = new audio
only, at a fraction of the cost.

**Fallback:** if after 10 episodes traction is weak, the same style key,
pipeline and channel can pivot to general history/science documentaries
(Option C) without rebuilding anything.
